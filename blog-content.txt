# Feature Specification: "Next Eligible Date" Calculator
### Website: LifeSaversUnited.org
### Version: 1.0
### Authority Reference: CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation), NBTC (National Blood Transfusion Council), MoHFW (Ministry of Health & Family Welfare), eRaktKosh Guidelines — India

---

## 1. OVERVIEW & PURPOSE

The "Next Eligible Date" feature is a self-service tool on LifeSaversUnited.org that helps blood donors in India find out when they are next eligible to donate — based on their last donation date and the type of donation they made. Additionally, it shows them when they become eligible for ALL other types of donations, not just the one they last did. To make it actionable, donors can set a calendar reminder directly from the results page for any donation type they choose.

The goal is to increase repeat donations by removing the guesswork from the process, keeping donors informed, and making it easy for them to commit to their next donation before they even leave the page.

---

## 2. WHO WILL USE THIS FEATURE

This feature is for any blood donor in India who has previously donated blood or blood components and wants to know:

- When they can safely donate again
- What types of donations they are eligible for and when
- How to set a reminder so they don't forget their next donation date

No login or account is required to use this feature. It is open to all visitors of the website.

---

## 3. WHAT THE USER NEEDS TO PROVIDE (INPUT FIELDS)

The feature will ask the donor to fill in two simple pieces of information:

**Field 1 — Last Donation Date**
The donor selects or types the date on which they last donated. This should be a date picker that allows selection of any past date. Future dates should be blocked. The system should also prevent dates older than 5 years, as such old data is not practically useful.

**Field 2 — Last Donation Type**
The donor selects from a dropdown menu what type of donation they made last time. The options, based on Indian guidelines, are:

- Whole Blood
- Single Donor Platelets (SDP / Plateletpheresis)
- Plasma (Apheresis)
- Double Red Cell (Apheresis)
- Packed Red Blood Cells (pRBC — component from whole blood)

A small tooltip or info icon should appear next to each option explaining what it means in simple language, because many donors may not know the technical name of the donation they made. For example, next to "Whole Blood" the tooltip can say: "The most common type — a full unit of blood collected from your arm in about 10–15 minutes."

**Optional Field 3 — Donor Gender**
Since Indian guidelines (NBTC) specify different waiting periods for male and female whole blood donors, the tool should ask for gender when "Whole Blood" is selected as the last donation type. The options should be Male and Female. This field only appears when Whole Blood is selected. For all other donation types, the waiting period is the same regardless of gender.

---

## 4. THE CORE LOGIC — WAITING PERIODS (INDIA / CDSCO / NBTC GUIDELINES)

The following waiting periods are based on official Indian blood donation guidelines issued by CDSCO, NBTC under MoHFW, and eRaktKosh (the national blood bank management system).

---

### 4A. WAITING PERIOD AFTER WHOLE BLOOD DONATION

If the donor's last donation was Whole Blood:

- Male donors must wait a minimum of 3 months (90 days) before donating Whole Blood again.
- Female donors must wait a minimum of 4 months (120 days) before donating Whole Blood again.
- Before donating Single Donor Platelets (SDP), any donor must wait a minimum of 4 weeks (28 days) after a Whole Blood donation.
- Before donating Plasma via Apheresis, the donor must wait a minimum of 4 weeks (28 days) after a Whole Blood donation.
- Double Red Cell donation requires a minimum wait of 6 months (180 days) after Whole Blood.

---

### 4B. WAITING PERIOD AFTER SINGLE DONOR PLATELETS (SDP / PLATELETPHERESIS)

If the donor's last donation was Single Donor Platelets:

- The donor may donate SDP again after a minimum of 2 days (48 hours). However, they must not exceed 24 SDP donations in a calendar year and not more than 2 per week.
- Before donating Whole Blood, the donor must wait a minimum of 4 weeks (28 days) after an SDP donation.
- Before donating Plasma via Apheresis, the donor must wait a minimum of 7 days after an SDP donation.
- Double Red Cell donation requires a minimum wait of 4 weeks (28 days) after SDP.

---

### 4C. WAITING PERIOD AFTER PLASMA (APHERESIS)

If the donor's last donation was Plasma via Apheresis:

- The donor may donate Plasma again after a minimum of 14 days (2 weeks). Plasma via Apheresis can be donated up to every 2 weeks.
- Before donating Whole Blood, the donor must wait a minimum of 4 weeks (28 days) after a Plasma donation.
- Before donating Single Donor Platelets (SDP), the donor must wait a minimum of 7 days after a Plasma donation.
- Double Red Cell donation requires a minimum wait of 4 weeks (28 days) after Plasma.

---

### 4D. WAITING PERIOD AFTER DOUBLE RED CELL (APHERESIS)

If the donor's last donation was Double Red Cell:

- The donor must wait a minimum of 6 months (180 days) before donating Double Red Cell again.
- Before donating Whole Blood, the donor must wait a minimum of 6 months (180 days).
- Before donating SDP, the donor must wait a minimum of 4 weeks (28 days).
- Before donating Plasma, the donor must wait a minimum of 4 weeks (28 days).

---

### 4E. WAITING PERIOD AFTER PACKED RED BLOOD CELLS (pRBC — Component)

This is when a donor donated whole blood and the component was separated at the blood bank. The waiting period logic is the same as Whole Blood donation, since the full volume of red blood cells was collected.

Apply the same rules as Whole Blood for this type.

---

## 5. WHAT THE SYSTEM WILL DISPLAY (OUTPUT / RESULTS SCREEN)

Once the donor submits their last donation date and type, the system calculates and displays a results card. This results card shows:

**Section A — Your Last Donation Summary**
A brief confirmation line at the top, for example:
"You last donated Whole Blood on 15 January 2025."

**Section B — Next Eligible Dates Table**
A clear table or card layout showing all donation types and when the donor can next donate each one. The table should have:

- Donation Type (e.g., Whole Blood, Single Donor Platelets, Plasma, Double Red Cell)
- Next Eligible Date (shown as a full date, e.g., 15 April 2025)
- Days Remaining (how many more days from today until eligible, e.g., "12 more days" OR "Eligible Today" OR "Already Eligible — you can donate now")
- Status indicator — a simple visual tag: GREEN for already eligible, ORANGE for eligible within 30 days, RED for more than 30 days away
- A "Set Reminder" button next to each row

If a donation type has the same eligible date as another, group them together with a note.

**Section C — Special Notice for SDP**
If the donor's last donation was SDP and they are an SDP donor, show a note:
"Per Indian guidelines, SDP can be donated up to 24 times per year and not more than twice per week. Please keep track of your annual donation count."

**Section D — Health Reminder Note**
At the bottom of the results, display a standard disclaimer in a small box:
"These dates are calculated based on NBTC/CDSCO guidelines for Indian blood donors. Final eligibility is always determined by the blood bank's medical officer on the day of donation through a health screening. Factors such as haemoglobin levels (minimum 12.5 g/dL), body weight (minimum 45–50 kg), blood pressure, pulse, and recent illnesses or medications may affect your eligibility on the day."

---

## 6. SET REMINDER FEATURE (CALENDAR INTEGRATION)

Next to each donation type in the results table, there is a "Set Reminder" button. When clicked, this opens a small popup or dropdown offering the following calendar options:

- Add to Google Calendar
- Add to Apple Calendar (downloads .ics file)
- Add to Outlook Calendar (downloads .ics file)
- Copy Date to Clipboard (plain text fallback)

**What the reminder should contain:**
- Title: "Time to Donate Blood — [Donation Type]" (e.g., "Time to Donate Blood — Whole Blood")
- Date: The exact eligible date calculated by the system
- Description: A short message such as — "You are now eligible to donate [Donation Type] as per Indian NBTC guidelines. Visit your nearest blood bank or book a donation camp at LifeSaversUnited.org."
- Optional Location field: Left blank, or pre-filled with "Nearest Blood Bank" as placeholder text
- Reminder alert: Set to trigger 1 day before the eligible date and again on the morning of the eligible date (for Google Calendar and Outlook integrations)

For Apple Calendar and Outlook, the system generates a standard .ics file that downloads automatically when the user clicks the option.

For Google Calendar, it opens a new browser tab with the Google Calendar event creation page pre-filled with all the details above using Google Calendar's URL parameters.

## 7. ADDITIONAL FEATURES & SMART BEHAVIOURS

**7A — "You Can Donate Today" Banner**
If the system calculates that the donor is already past their waiting period for any donation type, a prominent banner appears at the top of the results saying:
"Great news! You are already eligible to donate [Donation Type]. Find a blood bank near you today."
This banner should link to the blood bank locator on the website or to eRaktKosh's locator.

**7B — Share Results**
A "Share" button below the results card that allows the donor to share their next eligible dates with a friend or family member via WhatsApp, SMS, or copy link. This is useful to encourage peer motivation and group donations.

**7C — "Plan Ahead" Button**
Below the results, a button labeled "Plan My Next Donation" which, when clicked, opens a simple prompt asking "Would you like to schedule a donation reminder for your next eligible date?" and pre-selects the soonest eligible donation type as the default.

**7D — No Account Needed**
The results are displayed immediately without requiring the user to create an account or log in. However, if the user IS logged in, the system should optionally save their last donation record to their profile for future reference and auto-fill next time.

**7E — Mobile Responsive**
The entire feature — the input form, results table, and reminder popup — must be fully functional on mobile devices. Many donors in India access the web via smartphone. Buttons must be large enough to tap comfortably. The table should stack vertically on smaller screens.

**7F — Language Support (Future Scope)**
While the initial version should be in English, the feature should be built in a way that makes it easy to add Gujarati and Hindi language options in a future update, since LifeSaversUnited.org serves a primarily Gujarat-based community.

---

## 8. IMPORTANT EDGE CASES TO HANDLE

The system must handle these situations gracefully:

**Edge Case 1 — Date in the Future**
If the donor accidentally enters a future date as their last donation date, show an error: "Last donation date cannot be in the future. Please check and re-enter."

**Edge Case 2 — Already Eligible**
If the waiting period has already passed, do not show a negative number. Show "Already Eligible" in green instead of "-5 days."

**Edge Case 3 — Same Day Eligibility**
If the eligible date is today, show "Eligible Today" rather than 0 days.

**Edge Case 4 — SDP Annual Limit**
If the donor selects SDP as their last donation type, show a note below the SDP row: "Note: You are permitted a maximum of 24 SDP donations per year as per Indian guidelines. Please track your annual count."

**Edge Case 5 — Unknown Donation Type**
If a donor is unsure what type they donated, add a "Not Sure?" link near the dropdown that opens a simple one-page guide explaining the difference between donation types in plain, non-medical language with visual icons.

**Edge Case 6 — Very Old Donation Date**
If the last donation was more than 2 years ago, display all donation types as "Already Eligible" and add a note: "It has been a while since your last donation — your body has had plenty of time to recover. You should be eligible to donate any type now, subject to health screening on the day."

## 9. COMPLIANCE & MEDICAL DISCLAIMER

This feature is an informational tool only and is based on the following official Indian authorities and their published guidelines:

- CDSCO — Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation (under MoHFW)
- NBTC — National Blood Transfusion Council
- NACO — National AIDS Control Organisation
- eRaktKosh — National Blood Bank Management System (MoHFW)
- Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 and its Amendments — Schedule F Part XII B (Blood Bank Regulations)

The feature must display a medical disclaimer on the results page clarifying that:

1. The dates shown are minimum waiting periods as per national guidelines.
2. Final eligibility is always determined by a qualified medical officer at the blood bank on the day of donation.
3. Donors with specific health conditions, recent illnesses, surgeries, vaccinations, medications, or travel history may have additional deferral periods beyond what this tool shows.
4. The website is not a medical authority and this tool does not constitute medical advice.
5. Donors are encouraged to contact their nearest licensed blood bank for any questions about their specific eligibility.

## 10. SUMMARY TABLE — WAITING PERIODS AT A GLANCE

The following table summarises all the waiting periods this feature will use, based on Indian NBTC/CDSCO guidelines:

| Previous Donation Type | Wait Before Next Whole Blood | Wait Before Next SDP | Wait Before Next Plasma | Wait Before Next Double Red Cell |
|------------------------|------------------------------|----------------------|-------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Whole Blood (Male) | 90 days (3 months) | 28 days | 28 days | 180 days |
| Whole Blood (Female) | 120 days (4 months) | 28 days | 28 days | 180 days |
| Single Donor Platelets | 28 days | 2 days (max 24/year) | 7 days | 28 days |
| Plasma (Apheresis) | 28 days | 7 days | 14 days | 28 days |
| Double Red Cell | 180 days | 28 days | 28 days | 180 days |

---

## 11. WHAT THIS FEATURE IS NOT

To keep the scope clear, this version of the feature will NOT:

- Check or verify the donor's actual health status or haemoglobin levels
- Connect to any hospital or blood bank database to verify past donations
- Show real-time blood availability or urgency at nearby blood banks (that is a separate feature)
- Send automated SMS or email reminders (calendar integration handles this via the user's own calendar app)
- Replace the official screening done by the blood bank on donation day
